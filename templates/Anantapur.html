WELCOME TO ANANTHAPURAM DISTRICT

Anantapur District is known for its abundant Gold mines. Gold regularly happens in free natural (local) structure, as pieces or grains, in rocks, in veins, and alluvial deposits. Andhra Pradesh is well known for its mica and gold mines

Places to visit in Anantapuramu are

Kumbhakarna Theme Park.

As you approach Kumbhakarna Park near Penukonda, around 70 km from Anantapur, you'll find yourself stepping into a world where mythology comes alive. This unique attraction, nestled in the picturesque landscapes of Andhra Pradesh, offers a fascinating glimpse into the epic tales of the Ramayana.

Penukonda Dargah.

The Penukonda Dargah, also known as Dargah Hazrat Baba Fakhruddin (RA) Dargah & Masjid, is a significant Sufi shrine in Penukonda, Anantapur district, Andhra Pradesh, India, and is a revered place of worship, especially during the Urs festival in March.

Explore With Maps

Gooty Fort

Gooty, or Gutti as the locals refer to it, is unlike any fort you might encounter on your way to Anantapur. It's a citadel with a surprise inside. Often referred to as Ravadurg, it is a fort complex with 15 small forts inside. The fort is also enlisted as one of the centrally protected monuments of national importance.

Hemavathi Fort And Temples

Hemavathi, located 140 km from Anantapur, was the capital of the Pallava rulers in the 9th and 10th centuries and features a fort (now demolished) and temples showcasing Pallava and Chola era craftsmanship.

Kadiri Lakshmi Narasimha Swamy Temple

In Anantapur district, Andhra Pradesh, you can find the Kadiri Lakshmi Narasimha Swamy Temple, a significant site in Hindu mythology, and the historical Hemavathi Fort, along with other temples showcasing the region's rich heritage.

Kasapuram Anjaneya Swamy Temple

Kasapuram is the home of Nettikanti Veera Anjaneya Swamy's temple. A large number of Hindu pilgrims from the districts of Anantapur, Bellary, and Kurnool travel to Kasapuram to worship. On Saturdays, a considerable number of pilgrims visit the temple.

Lepakshi Veerabhadra Swamy Temple

The Lepakshi Temple, dedicated to Veerabhadra (a fierce form of Lord Shiva), is renowned for its unique architectural features, including the hanging pillar and the monolithic Nandi (bull), showcasing Vijayanagara-style architecture.
